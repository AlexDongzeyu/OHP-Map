About this project

This is a student memorial map of the Crestwood Oral History Project — interviews Crestwood Preparatory College students conducted with Holocaust survivors. Each survivor's testimony is shown as a journey across Europe: from the town they were born in, through the ghettos and camps they were forced through, to liberation and the new lives many built in Toronto.

How the journeys were made

Places are auto-extracted from each survivor's public archive summary and matched to a curated gazetteer of historical place-names (so "Lemberg" resolves to today's Lviv) before being geocoded once, at build time. Every record is pending human verification — these are pointers into the archive, not authoritative biographies. Uncertain dates are kept uncertain; survivors' own spellings of places are always preserved.

A note on care

This is a memorial, not a product. "Same place, same time" links between survivors are shown only as candidates — never a claim that two people met. Before any record is presented as fact, it must be checked against the full testimony and cleared with the project's teacher.
